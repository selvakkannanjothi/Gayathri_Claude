#### Main Crops:
Paddy · Wheat · Barley · Peas · Lentils · Sugarcane · Oil seeds · Mustard
South India: Pepper · Cardamom (mentioned specifically)

#### Taxes in Gupta Period:

| Tax | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Bhaga** | **1/6 of produce** → land revenue (main tax) |
| **Bhoga** | Periodic supply of fruits, firewood, flowers to king |
| **Kara** | Fixed tax on certain commodities |
| **Bali** | Religious offering turned into obligatory tax |
| **Udranga / Uparikara** | Additional levies |
| **Hiranya** | Tax paid in CASH (not kind) |
| **Sulka** | Customs / toll duty |
| **Halivakara** | Tax on plough — agricultural tax |
| **Vata-Bhuta** | Tax for supernatural beings (religious tax) |

> Key: **Bhaga = 1/6** of produce → most frequently asked tax

### Trade & Commerce

```
Traders — Two Types:
    ├── SRESTI = Settled merchant (shop-based, city trader)
    └── SARTHAVAHA = Caravan trader (long-distance, inter-regional)

Guilds (Shrenis):
    ├── Acted as BANKS (loaned money, took deposits)
    ├── Guild Chief + 2-3 Executive Officers
    └── Issued their own SEALS (from Vaishali)

Important Ports:
    ├── Tamralipti (Bengal coast) — eastern trade
    ├── Calliena / Kalyan (western coast)
    └── Chaul (western coast)
```

### Position of Women in Gupta Period

> ⚠️ **Source Note:** The 11th Std History textbook (Unit 7) does **not** have a dedicated section on women's position. The following is based only on what is directly mentioned in the textbook. This section will be expanded when a better source is added to Content/.

#### What the Textbook Says (Direct References Only):

**1. Fahien's Observation — Care for Widows (Pataliputra)**

> *"All the poor and destitute, orphans, widows and childless, maimed and cripples are provided with every kind of help."*

```
What this tells us:
  → Widows existed as a recognised social group (separate from others)
  → The STATE / Vaisya families set up charity houses in cities
  → Widows were listed alongside orphans, childless, maimed — suggesting
    they were considered vulnerable / needed external support
  → Fahien observed this specifically in Pataliputra (capital)
```

**2. Smritis — Social Laws Governing Life (Including Women)**

> ⚠️ The textbook mentions these Smritis governed social life but does **not** detail their specific content on women.

## SECTION 14 — Deep Dive

### Why is Gupta Period called "Golden Age"?

```
GOLDEN AGE — 5 Reasons

1. LITERATURE & LANGUAGE
   → Sanskrit became official language
   → Kalidasa — greatest Sanskrit poet/playwright
   → Ramayana, Mahabharata, 18 Puranas got final form

2. SCIENCE & MATHEMATICS
   → Aryabhatta: Zero, Decimal, Earth rotates on axis
   → Varahamihira: Encyclopedic knowledge
   → Brahmagupta: Advanced mathematics

3. ART & ARCHITECTURE
   → Ajanta cave paintings (Madhyadesa style)
   → Nagara & Dravida temple styles evolved
   → Mehrauli Iron Pillar (metallurgical marvel)

4. TRADE & ECONOMY
   → Land revenue system (Bhaga = fair 1/6)
   → Guild system (banking + insurance)
   → Both sea and land trade flourished

5. RELIGIOUS TOLERANCE
   → Hinduism + Buddhism + Jainism coexisted
   → No religious persecution under Gupta kings
```

### Samudragupta — Military Campaigns (Allahabad Pillar Evidence)

```
Samudragupta's Policy — "Aryavarta Policy" (north):
→ Defeated & annexed 9 northern kings completely

"Dakshinapatha Policy" (south):
→ Defeated 12 southern kings
→ BUT → released them back (not annexed)
→ Policy: "Capture-Release-Impress" = show of power

Result:
→ Empire = most of north India (direct control)
→ South + borders = tributary/feudatory states
```

### Decline of Gupta Empire — Causes

```
Decline Factors:
    ├── HUN INVASIONS (Hunas from Central Asia)
    │   → Skanda Gupta fought off temporarily
    │   → But repeated invasions weakened treasury & army
    │
    ├── WEAK SUCCESSORS after Skanda Gupta
    │   → No powerful king to hold empire together
    │
    ├── FEUDAL TENDENCIES
    │   → Governors/Uparikas became independent rulers
    │
    └── LOSS OF WESTERN TRADE ROUTES
        → Sea trade shifted; revenue fell
```
