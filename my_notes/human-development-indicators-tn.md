## SECTION 3 — HDI Calculation Flowchart [UPSC-P] [TNPSC]

```
HDI — HOW IT IS CALCULATED
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DIMENSION 1: HEALTH
  Indicator → Life Expectancy at Birth
  Min: 20 yrs | Max: 85 yrs
  → Health Dimension Index (0 to 1)

DIMENSION 2: EDUCATION
  Indicator A → Mean Years of Schooling (adults 25+)
  Indicator B → Expected Years of Schooling (children)
  → Education Dimension Index (0 to 1)

DIMENSION 3: STANDARD OF LIVING
  Indicator → GNI per capita (PPP $, 2017 constant prices)
  Min: $100 | Max: $75,000
  → Income Dimension Index (0 to 1)

          ↓           ↓           ↓
    HDI = ³√ (Health × Education × Income)
          [GEOMETRIC MEAN — NOT arithmetic mean ← UPSC trap]

HDI CATEGORIES:
  ≥ 0.800  → VERY HIGH Human Development   (e.g. Switzerland 0.967)
  0.700 – 0.799 → HIGH Human Development   (e.g. China 0.788)
  0.550 – 0.699 → MEDIUM Human Development ← INDIA (0.644)
  < 0.550  → LOW Human Development

NOTE: Before 2010, Education = Adult Literacy Rate + GER
      After 2010 (revised), Education = MYS + EYS ← UPSC-asked
```

### GDP Concepts (10th Economics Unit 1) [UPSC-P]

```
GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
= Total market value of all FINAL goods & services
  produced WITHIN a country in a given year (by residents + non-residents)

Key formula:
GDP = C + I + G + (X – M)
  C = Private Consumption
  I = Investment
  G = Government Expenditure
  X–M = Net Exports

CHAIN OF NATIONAL INCOME CONCEPTS:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GDP (at market price)
  → subtract Indirect Taxes, add Subsidies → GDP at Factor Cost
  → subtract Depreciation (Capital Consumption) → NDP
  → add Net Factor Income from Abroad → NNP = National Income
  → divide by Population → Per Capita Income

NOMINAL vs REAL GDP:
  Nominal GDP = calculated at current market prices
  Real GDP = adjusted for inflation (base year prices)
  GDP Deflator = (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) × 100

AT FACTOR COST vs AT MARKET PRICE:
  GDP at Market Price = GDP at Factor Cost + Indirect Taxes – Subsidies
```

### Multi-Dimensional Analysis

```
A. HEALTH DIMENSION — INDIA'S CHALLENGE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gap: IMR 28 (India) vs 14 (TN), 6 (Kerala), 6 (Sri Lanka)
Gap: MMR 97 (India) vs 54 (TN) vs global best ~3–5

  → Underinvestment: India spends ~1.5–2% GDP on health

  → Rural–urban gap: 70% population in rural; healthcare concentrated urban
  → Social determinants: malnutrition, sanitation, safe water access

B. EDUCATION DIMENSION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
India: MYS = 6.6 years (low) | Literacy = 77.7%

Challenge:
  → Learning outcomes: children in school but not learning

> → **H**aq + **A**martya **S**en → 1990
> → Pakistani economist + Indian Nobel laureate = HDI born

### HDI vs Arithmetic/Geometric

> **"HDI = GEO not ARITH"** (geo = geometric; never arithmetic)
> → Geometric mean gives EQUAL WEIGHT to all 3 dimensions
> → Arithmetic mean would allow a high income country to compensate low education

### State Rankings

> **"Keri Dil Hi TN"** (Kerala, Delhi, Himachal Pradesh, Tamil Nadu)
> → **K**e**r**ala → **D**elhi → H**i**machal → **T**amil **N**adu

### GDP Chain (10th Economics)

> **"GDP – D = NDP; NDP + N = NNP; NNP ÷ P = PCI"**
> - D = Depreciation
> - N = Net factor income from abroad
> - P = Population; PCI = Per Capita Income

## SECTION 13 — Deep Dive [UPSC-M]

### India's Economy: Pre and Post Independence (11th Economics Unit 8)

```
PRE-INDEPENDENCE ECONOMY
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STATE AT COLONIAL ENTRY (1600–1757):
  → India = ~24% of world GDP (Maddison data, 1700 CE)
  → Highly developed handicrafts, textiles, shipbuilding

COLONIAL EXPLOITATION:
  → Drain of Wealth theory: Dadabhai Naoroji ("Grand Old Man of India")
    Book: "Poverty and Un-British Rule in India" (1901)
    Estimated: ~£500 million drained annually
  → R.C. Dutt: "Economic History of India" — documented deindustrialisation
  → M.G. Ranade: critique of free trade policy

COLONIAL IMPACT:
  → Deindustrialisation: handloom weavers displaced (Manchester textiles)
  → Land systems: Zamindari (Bengal), Ryotwari (Madras, Bombay),
    Mahalwari (NW India) → peasant exploitation
  → Railways: built for British interest (raw material drain; troop movement)
  → Life expectancy at independence: ~32 years
  → Literacy at independence: ~18%
  → GDP share of world by 1947: ~4% (from 24% in 1700)

POST-INDEPENDENCE PHASES:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Phase 1 (1947–1991): MIXED ECONOMY + FIVE YEAR PLANS
    → Planning Commission: 1950
    → Nehruvian socialism: public sector dominance
    → Import substitution industrialisation
    → Green Revolution (1960s-70s): wheat + rice self-sufficiency
      → Norman Borlaug (wheat); M.S. Swaminathan (India's architect)
    → White Revolution (Operation Flood): Verghese Kurien (AMUL)
    → Heavy industry: steel plants, PSUs (Bhilai, Rourkela, Durgapur)

  Phase 2 (1991 onwards): LPG REFORMS
    → Trigger: Balance of Payments crisis (1991); $1 billion forex left
    → India mortgaged gold reserves → IMF conditions
    → Reform architects: PM P.V. Narasimha Rao + FM Dr. Manmohan Singh
    → Liberalisation: dismantled licence raj, MRTP Act
    → Privatisation: PSU disinvestment
    → Globalisation: FDI allowed; trade liberalisation
    → Impact: GDP growth accelerated; services (IT) boom; middle class expansion

  Phase 3 (2014 onwards): STRUCTURAL REFORMS
    → Make in India, Digital India, Startup India
    → GST (July 2017): "One Nation One Tax"
    → IBC (Insolvency + Bankruptcy Code): resolving stressed assets
    → UPI + Jan Dhan + Aadhaar = JAM Trinity: financial inclusion
    → India = 5th largest economy (2023); aiming for 3rd by 2030
```

### Indian Economy Structure (11th Economics Unit 7)

```
SECTORS OF ECONOMY
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRIMARY SECTOR (Agriculture, Mining, Forestry, Fishing):
  → India: ~17% GDP | ~45–50% employment
  → Disguised unemployment high (marginal productivity = 0)
  → TN: ~8–10% GSDP | ~40% employment

SECONDARY SECTOR (Manufacturing, Construction, Electricity):
  → India: ~28% GDP
  → TN: ~32–35% GSDP (strong manufacturing)

TERTIARY SECTOR (Services: IT, Finance, Trade, Transport):
  → India: ~55–57% GDP (largest)
  → TN: ~55–60% GSDP; Chennai = IT + finance + healthcare hub

STRUCTURAL TRANSFORMATION (10th Economics Unit 1):
  → Developed economies: shifted from Agriculture → Industry → Services
  → India: skipped manufacturing surge; jumped directly to services
  → "Premature deindustrialisation" concern
  → Agriculture still employs ~50% despite low GDP share
