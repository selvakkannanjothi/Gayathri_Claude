# PART 1 — VEDIC AGE (1500–600 BCE)

## SECTION 1.1 — Overview: Early Vedic vs Later Vedic [TNPSC] [UPSC-P]

| Feature | EARLY VEDIC (1500–1000 BCE) | LATER VEDIC (1000–600 BCE) |
|---------|----------------------------|---------------------------|
| Region | Punjab, Sindh (Sapta Sindhu) | Gangetic plains (east) |
| Key text | **Rig Veda** | Sama, Yajur, Atharva Vedas + Upanishads |
| Society | Tribal; semi-nomadic | Settled agriculture; caste rigid |
| Economy | Cattle-based; barter | Agriculture dominant; villages established |
| Polity | Tribal chiefs (Rajan) | Kings (territorial); Sabha & Samiti decline |
| Women | Relatively free (Gargi, Lopamudra) | Subordinate; restricted |
| Caste | 4 Varnas mentioned but flexible | Varnas became hereditary & rigid |
| Religion | Nature worship; Indra chief god | Vishnu, Shiva gain prominence |
| Iron | Not known | Iron (Syama Ayas) introduced |

## SECTION 1.2 — The Four Vedas [TNPSC] [UPSC-P]

```
VEDAS (meaning: "knowledge" from Sanskrit root "Vid")
    │
    ├── RIG VEDA ★
    │   → Oldest Veda; 1028 hymns in 10 Mandalas
    │   → Hymns to deities (Indra, Agni, Varuna, Soma)
    │   → Gayatri Mantra — in Rig Veda (Mandala 3)
    │   → Purusha Sukta — mentions 4 Varnas (Mandala 10)
    │
    ├── SAMA VEDA
    │   → "Veda of melodies" — musical chants
    │   → Priests: Udgatri (chanters)
    │   → Source of Indian classical music
    │
    ├── YAJUR VEDA
    │   → "Veda of rituals/sacrifices"
    │   → Prose + verse; sacrificial formulas
    │   → Priests: Adhvaryu
    │   → Divided into: Krishna Yajur Veda & Shukla Yajur Veda
    │
    └── ATHARVA VEDA ★
        → "Veda of charms/spells/magic"
        → Everyday life; medicine; folk religion
        → Least Vedic in character; added last
        → Priests: Brahma
```

### Vedic Literature Hierarchy

```
SHRUTI (heard — divine revelation)
    ├── 4 VEDAS (Rig, Sama, Yajur, Atharva)
    │       Each Veda has:
    │         ├── Samhita (hymns — core)
    │         ├── Brahmanas (ritual explanations — prose)
    │         ├── Aranyakas (forest texts — transition)
    │         └── Upanishads (philosophy — end of Veda = Vedanta)
    │
SMRITI (remembered — human composition)
    ├── 6 Vedangas (limbs of Vedas)
    │     ├── Shiksha (phonetics)
    │     ├── Kalpa (ritual)
    │     ├── Vyakarana (grammar — Panini's Ashtadhyayi)
    │     ├── Nirukta (etymology — Yaska)
    │     ├── Chandas (metre — Pingala)
    │     └── Jyotisha (astronomy)
    ├── Puranas (18 Mahapuranas)
    ├── Epics (Ramayana, Mahabharata)
    └── Dharmashastras (Smritis — Manu, Yajnavalkya)
```

## SECTION 1.3 — Vedic Society [TNPSC] [UPSC-P] [UPSC-M]

### Four Varnas (Purusha Sukta, Rig Veda 10th Mandala)

```
BRAHMA (Cosmic Being / Purusha)
         │
    ┌────┴──────────────────────────┐
    │                               │
 BRAHMANAS              KSHATRIYAS (Rajanyas)
 (mouth of Brahma)      (arms of Brahma)
 Priests, teachers      Warriors, rulers
         │                               │
    VAISHYAS                         SHUDRAS
   (thighs)                          (feet)
   Traders, farmers,                 Servants
   cattle-herders
```

> ⚠️ Early Vedic: Varna = occupation-based (changeable)
> Later Vedic: Varna = birth-based (rigid, hereditary)
> Purusha Sukta = FIRST MENTION of 4 Varnas in literature

### Vedic Polity

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Rajan** | Tribal chief/king |
| **Jana** | Tribe/people |
| **Vish** | Clan within tribe |
| **Grama** | Village (smaller unit) |
| **Sabha** | Assembly of elders; restricted |
| **Samiti** | General tribal assembly; larger |
| **Vidatha** | Earliest tribal assembly (Rig Vedic) |
| **Purohita** | Royal priest — most important minister |
| **Senani** | Commander of army |
| **Gramani** | Village headman |
| **Spies (Spash/Dutas)** | Intelligence network |

### Vedic Economy

| Aspect | Early Vedic | Later Vedic |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Main activity | Cattle herding | Agriculture (plough — sira) |
| Metal | Copper/Bronze (Ayas) | Iron (Syama Ayas = black metal = iron) |
| Exchange | Barter (cattle = unit of value) | Nishka (gold coins/ornaments) |
| Trade | Limited | Growing; Vaishyas dominant |
| Villages | Temporary | Permanent, settled |

### Vedic Religion

| Early Vedic | Later Vedic |
|------------|-------------|
| Nature gods — Indra (thunder/war), Varuna (cosmic order), Agni (fire), Soma (plant deity) | Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva gain prominence |
| Indra — most hymns in Rig Veda | Indra loses importance |
| Sacrifices (Yajna) — simple | Elaborate sacrifices: Ashvamedha, Rajasuya, Vajapeya |
| No idol worship | Idol worship starts |
| No temples | Temples not yet; household fires sacred |

### Key Vedic Rituals (UPSC-M)

| Ritual | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Ashvamedha** | Horse sacrifice — assertion of sovereignty; king releases horse; war follows if stopped |
| **Rajasuya** | Coronation sacrifice; king's consecration |
| **Vajapeya** | Chariot race + soma sacrifice; renewal of royal power |
| **Agnishtoma** | Soma sacrifice for prosperity |
| **Purusha Medha** | Human sacrifice (symbolic; mentioned, rarely performed) |

## SECTION 1.4 — Key Upanishads & Vedanta [UPSC-P] [UPSC-M]

```
UPANISHADS (Vedanta = end of Vedas)
→ 108 Upanishads exist; 12 principal ones
→ Core Philosophy: BRAHMAN (universal soul) = ATMAN (individual soul)
→ Concept: Karma, Samsara (rebirth), Moksha (liberation)

PRINCIPAL UPANISHADS:
    ├── Brihadaranyaka (largest; Yajnavalkya's teachings)
    ├── Chandogya (largest philosophical work)
    ├── Mandukya (shortest; Om / AUM philosophy)
    ├── Katha (Nachiketa & Yama — story of death)
    └── Isha, Kena, Prashna, Mundaka, Taittiriya...

KEY CONCEPTS:
    ├── BRAHMAN = Ultimate reality, universal soul
    ├── ATMAN = Individual soul
    ├── TAT TVAM ASI = "That thou art" — Chandogya Upanishad
    ├── KARMA = Action determines next life
    ├── SAMSARA = Cycle of rebirth
    └── MOKSHA = Liberation from Samsara
```

## SECTION 2.2 — Rise of Magadha [TNPSC] [UPSC-P] [UPSC-M]

### Why Magadha became most powerful?

```
REASONS FOR MAGADHA'S RISE
    │
    ├── GEOGRAPHY
    │   → Two rivers: Son + Ganga → natural moat defense
    │   → Iron ore in Chota Nagpur → strong weapons
    │   → Fertile Gangetic plains → agricultural surplus
    │   → Forests → elephants for warfare
    │
    ├── CAPITALS (shifted for strategic reasons)
    │   Rajagriha → Pataliputra (Ajatashatru's time; at Ganga-Son confluence)
    │
    ├── POWERFUL DYNASTIES
    │   Haryanka → Shishunaga → Nanda → Mauryan
    │
    └── MILITARY INNOVATIONS
        → Use of elephants (Gajasena)
        → Iron weapons (first to use extensively)
        → Mahapadma Nanda's massive 200,000-strong army
```

### Magadha Dynasty Sequence

```
HARYANKA DYNASTY (544–412 BCE)
    ├── Bimbisara (544–492 BCE)
    │   → Contemporary of Buddha & Mahavira
    │   → First great king of Magadha; matrimonial alliances
    │   → Expanded by conquest + marriage (Lichchavi, Kosala, Madra)
    │   → Killed by his son Ajatashatru
    │
    └── Ajatashatru (492–460 BCE)
        → Killed his father Bimbisara (patricide)
        → Defeated Vaishali (Vajji Confederacy) — used Rathamusala (chariot with mace) & Mahasilakantaka (catapult)
        → Buddhist — attended Buddha's last days
        ↓
SHISHUNAGA DYNASTY (412–344 BCE)
    → Shishunaga: Destroyed Avanti (Ujjain) — Magadha's biggest rival
    → Moved capital to Vaishali temporarily
    → Succeeded by Kalashoka
        ↓
NANDA DYNASTY (344–321 BCE) ★
    ├── Mahapadma Nanda (first)
    │   → "Ekrat" — sole king; destroyed all Kshatriya rulers
    │   → First non-Kshatriya (Shudra origin) to rule empire
    │   → 200,000 infantry, 20,000 cavalry, 3000 elephants
    └── Dhana Nanda (last)
        → Alexander's army refused to cross Beas river due to fear of Nanda army
        → Overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya (with Chanakya's help) — 321 BCE
```

### Alexander's India Campaign

```
ALEXANDER IN INDIA (327–325 BCE)
    │
    ├── Entered via KHYBER PASS (327 BCE)
    ├── Conquered TAXILA (king Ambhi/Ambhiraj surrendered)
    ├── BATTLE OF HYDASPES (Jhelum river), 326 BCE
    │   → Defeated King PORUS (Paurava)
    │   → Alexander impressed by Porus's courage
    │   → Restored Porus's kingdom as vassal
    ├── Reached BEAS RIVER (Hyphasis)
    │   → Army MUTINIED — refused to go further (feared Nanda army)
    │   → Alexander turned back
    ├── Returned via Indus route; died in BABYLON (323 BCE)
    │
    IMPACT ON INDIA:
    → Direct contact between India & Greek world
    → Greeks left behind: coins, art (Gandhara School)
    → Cleared NW India of small kingdoms → made way for Mauryan Empire
    → Greek accounts (Megasthenes, Nearchus, Arrian) = valuable sources
```

# PART 3 — BUDDHISM & JAINISM

## SECTION 3.1 — Buddhism [TNPSC] [UPSC-P] [UPSC-M]

### Gautama Buddha — Life Facts

```
GAUTAMA BUDDHA
    ├── Birth name: SIDDHARTHA GAUTAMA
    ├── Born: 563 BCE at LUMBINI (Nepal)
    ├── Clan: SHAKYA (Kshatriya)
    ├── Father: Shuddhodana (king of Kapilavastu)
    ├── Mother: Mahamaya (died at birth); raised by Prajapati Gautami
    ├── Wife: YASHODHARA
    ├── Son: RAHULA
    ├── Great Renunciation (Mahabhinishkramana): Left home at 29
    │
    ├── ENLIGHTENMENT:
    │   → Meditated under PEEPAL TREE (Bodhi Tree)
    │   → Location: BODHGAYA (Gaya, Bihar)
    │   → Age: 35 years
    │   → Became "Buddha" = The Enlightened One
    │
    ├── FIRST SERMON (Dharmachakra Pravartan):
    │   → Location: DEER PARK, SARNATH (near Varanasi)
    │   → Audience: 5 monks (Panchabhadravargiya)
    │   → Taught: Four Noble Truths + Eightfold Path
    │
    └── DEATH (Mahaparinirvana):
        → 483 BCE at KUSHINAGAR (UP)
        → Age: 80 years
        → Cause: Illness (pork/mushroom meal — disputed)
```

### Four Noble Truths (Arya Satya) + Eightfold Path

```
FOUR NOBLE TRUTHS (Chatur Arya Satya)
    1. DUKKHA — Life is suffering/sorrow
    2. SAMUDAYA — Desire/craving is the cause of suffering
    3. NIRODHA — Suffering can be ended (by ending desire)
    4. MARGA — Eightfold Path leads to end of suffering

EIGHTFOLD PATH (Ashtangika Marga) — "Middle Path"
    ├── Right Understanding (Samma Ditthi)
    ├── Right Thought (Samma Sankappa)
    ├── Right Speech (Samma Vacha)
    ├── Right Action (Samma Kammanta)
    ├── Right Livelihood (Samma Ajiva)
    ├── Right Effort (Samma Vayama)
    ├── Right Mindfulness (Samma Sati)
    └── Right Concentration (Samma Samadhi)

→ Middle Path = Avoid extreme luxury AND extreme asceticism
→ "Madhyama Pratipad" = The Middle Way
```

### Buddhist Councils [TNPSC HIGH PRIORITY]

| Council | Date | Place | King | Significance |
|---------|------|-------|------|-------------|
| **1st Council** | 483 BCE | **Rajagriha** (Saptaparni Cave) | **Ajatashatru** | Compiled Vinaya Pitaka & Sutta Pitaka; president: Mahakassapa |
| **2nd Council** | 383 BCE | **Vaishali** | **Kalashoka** | Split between Sthaviravadins & Mahasanghikas; president: Sabakami |
| **3rd Council** | 250 BCE | **Pataliputra** | **Ashoka** | Compiled Abhidhamma Pitaka; completed Tripitaka; sent missionaries; president: Moggaliputta Tissa |
| **4th Council** | 1st CE | **Kundalvana (Kashmir)** | **Kanishka** | Split into Hinayana & Mahayana; president: Vasumitra; Ashvaghosha attended |

### Buddhist Scriptures — Tripitaka

```
TRIPITAKA ("Three Baskets") — Pali language
    │
    ├── VINAYA PITAKA
    │   → Rules for monks and nuns (Sangha discipline)
    │   → Compiled: 1st Buddhist Council
    │
    ├── SUTTA PITAKA
    │   → Buddha's teachings & discourses
    │   → Includes: Dhammapada, Jataka tales
    │   → Compiled: 1st Buddhist Council
    │
    └── ABHIDHAMMA PITAKA
        → Philosophical analysis of Dharma
        → Compiled: 3rd Buddhist Council (under Ashoka)

Jataka Tales → 547 stories of Buddha's previous lives
Dhammapada → Most read Buddhist text; collection of Buddha's sayings
```

### Schools of Buddhism

| School | Also Called | Key Features |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Theravada / Sthaviravada** | Hinayana ("Lesser Vehicle") | Original/orthodox; Pali Canon; individual salvation; no idol worship; strong in Sri Lanka, SE Asia |
| **Mahayana** | "Greater Vehicle" | Boddhisattva concept (helping others to achieve nirvana); Sanskrit texts; idol worship; spread to China, Japan, Korea |
| **Vajrayana** | Tantric Buddhism | Magic/rituals added; "thunderbolt vehicle"; strong in Tibet |

> **Boddhisattva** = One who delays own nirvana to help all sentient beings attain it

## SECTION 3.2 — Jainism [TNPSC] [UPSC-P]

### Mahavira — Life Facts

```
VARDHAMANA MAHAVIRA
    ├── Born: 599 BCE (traditional) / 540 BCE (modern scholars)
    ├── Place: KUNDAGRAMA (near Vaishali, Bihar)
    ├── Clan: LICCHAVI (Kshatriya)
    ├── Father: Siddhartha (head of Jnatrika clan)
    ├── Mother: Trishala (Lichchavi princess)
    ├── Wife: YASHODA | Daughter: ANOJJA (Priyadarshana)
    │
    ├── Renunciation: 30 years; joined Parshva sect initially
    ├── ENLIGHTENMENT (Kevala Jnana):
    │   → 12 years 6 months 15 days of severe asceticism
    │   → Under Sala tree at JRIMBHIKAGRAMA (Bihar)
    │   → Age: 42 years
    │   → Title: Mahavira (Great Hero), Jina (Conqueror), Nirgrantha
    │
    └── DEATH (Nirvana):
        → 527 BCE at PAVAPURI (near Rajagriha, Bihar)
        → Age: 72 years
```

### 24 Tirthankaras (UPSC-P)

```
Jainism recognizes 24 TIRTHANKARAS (Ford-Makers / Spiritual Teachers)

1st Tirthankara: RISHABHANATHA (Adinath)
    → Depicted with Bull symbol
23rd Tirthankara: PARSHVANATHA ★
    → Historical figure; 250 years before Mahavira
    → Gave 4 vows (Mahavira added 5th — Brahmacharya)
24th Tirthankara: MAHAVIRA ★★★
    → Most important; reformer of Jainism; contemporary of Buddha
```

### Five Vows of Jainism (Panchamahavrata)

| Vow | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Ahimsa** | Non-violence (highest virtue) |
| **Satya** | Non-lying (truth) |
| **Asteya** | Non-stealing |
| **Aparigraha** | Non-possessiveness |
| **Brahmacharya** | Celibacy (added by Mahavira; Parshva had first 4) |

### Jain Philosophy

```
JAIN CORE CONCEPTS
    ├── AHIMSA = Supreme moral rule; even breathing filtered to avoid killing insects
    ├── ANEKANTAVADA = "Many-sidedness of truth" / Relativity of knowledge
    │   → No single view is complete truth
    │   → Also called SYADVADA (maybe-ism)
    ├── TRIRATNA (Three Jewels):
    │   ├── Samyak Darshana = Right faith
    │   ├── Samyak Jnana = Right knowledge
    │   └── Samyak Charitra = Right conduct
    └── KARMA = Material substance attached to soul; must shed karma to achieve liberation
```

### Jain Sects

| Sect | Monks | Scripture | Language | Origin |
|------|-------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Digambara** ("Sky-clad") | Naked (no clothes) | Own canon | Ardha-Magadhi | Chandraguptagiri — associated with Chandragupta Maurya |
| **Shvetambara** ("White-clad") | White clothes | Agamas (12 Angas) | Ardha-Magadhi | More in Gujarat, Rajasthan |

> Split cause: Bhadrabahu (Digambara leader) led Jains to South India during famine; Sthulabuddhi led those who stayed; disagreement on clothing & texts

## SECTION 3.3 — Buddhism vs Jainism — Comparison [UPSC-M]

| Feature | Buddhism | Jainism |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Founder | Gautama Buddha | 24th Tirthankara Mahavira |
| Born | 563 BCE, Lumbini | 599 BCE, Kundagrama |
| Died | 483 BCE, Kushinagar | 527 BCE, Pavapuri |
| Key concept | Four Noble Truths; Middle Path | Ahimsa; Anekantavada; Triratna |
| God | No personal God; silence on Brahman | No creator God; focus on Tirthankaras |

| Language | **Pali** (common people's language) | **Ardha-Magadhi** (common people's language) |
| Asceticism | Moderate (Middle Path) | **Extreme asceticism** (Sallekhana — fasting to death) |
| Spread | International (SE Asia, China, Japan) | Mainly India (Gujarat, Rajasthan, Karnataka) |
