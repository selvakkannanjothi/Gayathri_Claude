## Human Rights — At a Glance

```
Human Rights
     │
     ├── Defined by UNO → "Rights inherent to ALL human beings
     │   regardless of race, gender, nationality, ethnicity,
     │   language, religion or any other status"
     │
     ├── Human Rights Day → December 10 (every year)
     │
     └── UNO formed → October 24, 1945 (after World War II)
```

### UDHR — Universal Declaration of Human Rights

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Proclaimed by | UN General Assembly, **Paris** |
| Date | **10 December 1948** |
| Resolution No. | **217A** |
| Articles | **30 Articles** |
| Coverage | Freedom of expression + civil, political, social, economic, cultural rights |

### Types of Human Rights

| Type | What it Covers |
|------|---------------|
| **Social Rights** | Full participation in society |
| **Economic Rights** | Right to meet basic economic needs |
| **Cultural Rights** | Enjoyment of culture with equality & dignity |
| **Civil Rights** | Basic rights from government laws — regardless of race, caste, gender |
| **Political Rights** | Right to participate in government formation & administration |

## Fundamental Rights — The 6 Rights

```
PART III (Articles 12–35) → 6 Fundamental Rights

1. Right to Equality
2. Right to Freedom
3. Right Against Exploitation
4. Right to Freedom of Conscience & Religion
5. Cultural & Educational Rights
6. Right to Constitutional Remedies  ← "Heart & Soul of Constitution" (Ambedkar)
```

### 1. Right to Equality

- Equality before law & equal protection of law
- **No discrimination** on grounds of: Religion | Caste | Race | Gender | Place of Birth

---

### 2. Right to Freedom — 6 Freedoms

| # | Freedom |
|---|---------|
| 1 | Speech and expression |
| 2 | Assemble peacefully **without arms** |
| 3 | Form associations and unions |
| 4 | Move freely throughout India |
| 5 | Reside and settle in **any part** of India |
| 6 | Practice any profession / occupation / trade / business |

> 🧠 **Memory Tip:** **SAMRPP** → Speech, Assemble, Move, Reside, Profession, Practice

---

### 3. Right Against Exploitation

- No child below **14 years** employed in mines, factories or hazardous work
- No forced labour / bonded labour
- No human trafficking

---

### 4. Right to Freedom of Conscience & Religion

- Freedom to **follow, practice and propagate** any religion
- Freedom of conscience — follow own ways of belief

---

### 5. Cultural & Educational Rights

- Right to **preserve, protect and promote** own culture
- Minorities can open schools/associations for education
- **No denial of admission** based on caste, colour, creed or religion

---

### 6. Right to Constitutional Remedies

- If any Fundamental Right is denied → approach **court**
- Court issues **WRITS** to restore rights

### Types of Writs

| Writ | Meaning | Used When |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Habeas Corpus** | "Produce the body" | Illegal detention |
| **Mandamus** | "We command" | When public authority fails duty |
| **Prohibition** | Stop lower court | Lower court exceeds jurisdiction |
| **Certiorari** | Transfer & quash | To quash wrong order of lower court |
| **Quo Warranto** | "By what authority" | Challenge to hold a public office |

> 🧠 **Memory Tip:** **H M P C Q** → **H**ave **M**any **P**owers? **C**heck **Q**ualification

---

### Difference: Human Rights vs Fundamental Rights

| Human Rights | Fundamental Rights |
|---|---|
| Inherent to all human beings | Rights of citizens listed in Constitution |
| Absolute — cannot be taken away | Basic rights for normal life |
| Recognised at **international** level | Guaranteed under **Constitution** of country |
| UN standard — like Directive Principles | Enforceable under law |

## Fundamental Duties — Article 51-A

> **Original Constitution (1950)** had NO Fundamental Duties
> Added by → **42nd Amendment Act, 1976** (during Indira Gandhi's govt)
> Total → **11 Duties**

### The 11 Fundamental Duties

| # | Duty |
|---|------|
| 1 | Respect Constitution, National Flag & National Anthem |
| 2 | Follow noble ideals of National Freedom Struggle |
| 3 | Uphold sovereignty, unity & integrity of India |
| 4 | Defend the country, render national service when called |
| 5 | Promote harmony & brotherhood; renounce practices derogatory to women |
| 6 | Value & preserve rich heritage of composite culture |
| 7 | Protect natural environment — forests, lakes, rivers, wildlife |
| 8 | Develop scientific temper, humanism, spirit of inquiry |
| 9 | Safeguard public property, abjure violence |
| 10 | Strive for excellence in all spheres of individual & collective activity |
| 11 | Provide education to child/ward up to **age 14** (by parent/guardian) |

> 🧠 **Memory Tip for 11 duties:** **FUDS-PVSE-PE**
> **F**lag, **U**phold, **D**efend, **S**overeignty, **P**romote, **V**alue, **S**cientific, **E**nvironment, **P**roperty, **E**xcellence + Education (11th)

## NHRC — National Human Rights Commission

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Established | **12 October 1993** |
| Under Act | Protection of Human Rights Act, **1993** |
| Location | **New Delhi** |
| Chairman | Former Chief Justice of India |
| Members | 3 from Judiciary + 4 from other departments |
| Function | Protect & promote human rights in India |

### NHRC Functions

```
NHRC Can →
  ✅ Inquire into violation of human rights
  ✅ Intervene in court proceedings related to HR
  ✅ Recommend compensation to victims
  ✅ Approach Supreme Court / High Court for directions
  ✅ Research & education in human rights
  ❌ Cannot punish violators directly
  ❌ Recommendations NOT binding (but must be replied within 1 month)
```

## Key Amendments to Remember

| Amendment | Year | Change |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **42nd Amendment** | **1976** | Added Fundamental Duties (Article 51-A) |
| **44th Amendment** | **1978** | Right to Property removed from FR → became legal right (Article 300-A) |
| **86th Amendment** | **2002** | Added 11th Fundamental Duty (education for children) + Article 21-A (Right to Education) |

## Exam Tips

- Right to Property was a **Fundamental Right** originally → removed by **44th Amendment 1978** → now a **Legal Right** under Article 300-A
- Right to Constitutional Remedies → **Article 32** → can approach **Supreme Court** directly
- **Article 226** → High Court can also issue writs
- Fundamental Duties are **NOT justiciable** (cannot be enforced by court)
- **11th Duty** (Education) was added by **86th Amendment 2002** — not part of original 42nd Amendment list
- NHRC Chairman must be a **retired Chief Justice of India**

## 📖 Deep Dive — Reasonable Restrictions on Fundamental Rights

> **Key Rule:** Fundamental Rights are NOT absolute.
> The State can impose **reasonable restrictions** — but ONLY on grounds mentioned in the Constitution itself.

---

### Article 19 — 6 Freedoms & Their Restrictions

| # | Freedom (Article) | Restrictions Allowed Under |
|---|-------------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Speech & Expression** — 19(1)(a) | 19(2) |
| 2 | **Assemble Peacefully** — 19(1)(b) | 19(3) |
| 3 | **Form Associations/Unions** — 19(1)(c) | 19(4) |
| 4 | **Move Freely** throughout India — 19(1)(d) | 19(5) |
| 5 | **Reside & Settle** anywhere — 19(1)(e) | 19(5) |
| 6 | **Practice Profession/Trade/Business** — 19(1)(g) | 19(6) |

---

#### 1. Freedom of Speech & Expression → Restrictions [Article 19(2)]

| Restriction Grounds | Added By |
|---------------------|----------|
| Sovereignty & Integrity of India | 16th Amendment, 1963 |
| Security of the State | Original |
| Friendly relations with foreign states | 1st Amendment, 1951 |
| Public Order | 1st Amendment, 1951 |
| Decency or Morality | Original |
| Contempt of Court | Original |
| Defamation | Original |
| Incitement to an Offence | 1st Amendment, 1951 |

> 🧠 **Memory Tip — 8 grounds:** **SSFPDCDI**
> **S**overeignty, **S**ecurity, **F**riendly relations, **P**ublic order, **D**ecency, **C**ontempt, **D**efamation, **I**ncitement

**What Freedom of Speech INCLUDES (Supreme Court rulings):**
- Freedom of press
- Right to propagate one's views
- Freedom of commercial advertisement
- Right to telecast (govt has no monopoly on electronic media)
- Right against phone tapping
- Right against bundh called by political parties
- Right to fly national flag
- Right to access internet (Anuradha Bhasin case, 2020)
- Right to post on social media

---

#### 2. Freedom of Assembly → Restrictions [Article 19(3)]

- Sovereignty & Integrity of India
- Public Order

> ❌ Does NOT include: right to strike | right to hold assembly on private land | armed assemblies

---

#### 3. Freedom of Association → Restrictions [Article 19(4)]

- Sovereignty & Integrity of India
- Public Order
- Morality

> ❌ Trade unions have NO guaranteed right to: effective bargaining | strike | lock-out

---

#### 4 & 5. Freedom of Movement & Residence → Restrictions [Article 19(5)]

- Interests of general public
- Protection of interests of **Scheduled Tribes**

> Note: **Internal movement** → Article 19 | **External movement** (going abroad, returning) → Article 21

---

#### 6. Freedom of Profession/Trade/Business → Restrictions [Article 19(6)]

- Interests of general public
- State can prescribe professional/technical qualifications
- State can carry on any trade/business as monopoly (complete or partial), excluding citizens

| Right against Exploitation | 23–24 | No trafficking, forced labour; No child labour below **14 years** |

### Article 20 — Protection Against Conviction (3 Protections)

```
Article 20
    │
    ├── No Ex-Post Facto Law
    │   → Cannot punish for an act that was NOT an offence when committed
    │   → Cannot give penalty GREATER than what existed at time of offence
    │
    ├── No Double Jeopardy
    │   → Cannot be prosecuted & punished for SAME offence twice
    │
    └── No Self-Incrimination
        → Cannot be FORCED to be a witness against oneself
```

> ✅ Available to both **citizens and non-citizens**
> ❌ Does NOT apply to civil laws or tax laws (only criminal laws)

---

### Article 21 — Right to Life & Personal Liberty

- Most important Fundamental Right
- Available to **both citizens and foreigners**
- **Maneka Gandhi case (1978):** Procedure must be **reasonable, fair and just** — not arbitrary
- Includes: Right to livelihood | Right to privacy | Right to dignity | Right to health | Right to education | Right to speedy trial

**Article 21-A** (added by 86th Amendment 2002):
- Free and compulsory education for children **aged 6–14 years**

---

### Article 22 — Protection on Arrest

| Protection | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Must be informed of reason of arrest | Immediately |
| Right to consult a lawyer | Of own choice |
| Produced before magistrate | Within **24 hours** of arrest |
| Cannot be detained beyond 24 hours | Without magistrate's order |

> ❌ Exceptions: Enemy aliens | Persons under Preventive Detention Law

### Key Constitutional Doctrines (Deep Concepts)

| Doctrine | Meaning | Article |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Judicial Review** | Courts can strike down laws violating FRs | Art 13 |
| **Rule of Law** | No person above the law; equality before law | Art 14 |
| **Basic Structure** | Parliament cannot alter the basic features of Constitution | Kesavananda Bharati case, 1973 |
| **Doctrine of Severability** | Only the unconstitutional part of a law is void, not entire law | Art 13 |
