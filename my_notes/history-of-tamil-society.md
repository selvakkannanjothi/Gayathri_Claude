## SECTION 2 — Pre-Historic Tamil People [TNPSC] [UPSC-P]

### Timeline of Cultures in Tamil Nadu

```
Palaeolithic (Lower)   → 20,00,000 – 8,000 BCE
    • Hand axes, cleavers
    • Athirampakkam (Kosasthalaiyar river) — 1.5–2 million years old
    • First discovered by Sir Robert Bruce Foote at PALLAVARAM, 1863
    • Called: "Madras Stone Tool Industry"
    • Species: Homo erectus
         ↓
Mesolithic              → 8,000 – 1,300 BCE
    • Microliths (small stone tools)
    • Hunting & gathering
    • Teri sites near Thoothukudi
    • Tamil Nadu–Sri Lanka connected before 5000 BCE (low sea level)
         ↓
Neolithic               → 2,000 – 1,000 BCE
    • Polished stone axes (celts) = main tool
    • Cattle rearing, cultivation of crops
    • Key site: PAYYAMPALLI (Vellore dist.) — earliest animal domestication
    • Crops: Horse gram, green gram
         ↓
Iron Age / Megalithic   → 1,300 – 300 BCE
    • Large stone burials (megalithic monuments)
    • Iron technology, Black & Red ware pottery
    • Key site: ADICHANALLUR (Tirunelveli dist.)
    • Foundation for Sangam Age
         ↓
Early Historic / Sangam → 300 BCE – 300 CE
    • Monarchies: Chera, Chola, Pandya
    • Classical Tamil literature
    • Urbanization, overseas trade
```

### Key Prehistoric Sites

| Site | District | Significance |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Athirampakkam** | Chennai region | Oldest stone tools in India (1.5–2 million years) |
| **Pallavaram** | Chennai | First Palaeolithic tool found by Robert Bruce Foote (1863) |
| **Payyampalli** | Vellore | Earliest Neolithic village in Tamil Nadu |
| **Adichanallur** | Tirunelveli | Iron Age / megalithic burial site; rice grains in burial urns |
| **Korkai** | Thoothukudi | Sangam-period port; pearl fishery; Tamil-Brahmi potsherds |

## SECTION 4 — Sangam Age [TNPSC] [UPSC-P]

### Three Great Kingdoms (Muvendars)

| Kingdom | Region | Capital | Symbol | Notable Ruler |
|---------|--------|---------|--------|--------------|
| **Cheras** | Central/North Travancore, Cochin, S. Malabar, Kongu | Vanji (Karur) | Bow & Arrow | Cheran Senguttuvan (introduced Pattini cult) |
| **Cholas** | Kaveri delta → Venkatam (Tirupathi) | Uraiyur, Puhar | Tiger | Karikalan (built Kallanai dam) |
| **Pandyas** | Present-day southern Tamil Nadu | Madurai, Korkai | Fish | Nedunchezhiyan (lord of Korkai) |

### Sangam Polity

```
KING (Ko / Kon / Vendan / Mannan)
    │
    ├── Crown Prince: Komahan
    ├── Court: Arasavai (king sat on Ariyanai throne)
    ├── Daily durbar: Naalavai
    ├── Officials: Aimperunguzhu (5-member) + Enberaayam (8-member)
    ├── Army: Padai — 4 divisions: Infantry, Cavalry, Elephants, Chariot
    │       Chief: Thanaithalaivan
    │       Weapons: Sword, Kedayam (shield), Tomaram (lance)
    │
    ├── Revenue:
    │   Main: Land tax = IRAI
    │   Others: Sungam (customs/toll), Tributes, Fines
    │
    └── Administration:
        Mandalam → Nadu → Kurram → Ur (village)
        Pattinam = coastal town | Puhar = harbour town
```

### Thinai — Five Eco-Regions

| Thinai | Landscape | Deity | Theme |
|--------|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Kurinji** | Hills | Murugan | Lovers meeting (Punarthal) |
| **Mullai** | Forest / pastoral | Thirumal (Vishnu) | Waiting (Iruthal) |
| **Marutham** | Agricultural plains | Indiran | Lovers' quarrel (Idal) |
| **Neythal** | Coastal | Varunan | Wailing (Irangal) |
| **Paalai** | Arid / wasteland | Kotravai | Separation (Pirithal) |

> Marutham (fertile) = menpulam; others (except Neythal) = vanpulam (dry)

### Sangam Society

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Women** | No social restrictions; 40 women poets; self-choice marriage; equal property rights; chastity (karpu) = highest virtue |
| **Women poets** | Avvaiyar, VelliVeethiyar, Kakkaipadiniyar, Aath Manthiyar, PonMudiyar |
| **Religion** | Primary deity: Murugan (Seyon); also: Sivan, Mayon (Vishnu), Indiran, Varunan, Kotravai; Hero stone (natukkal) worship; Buddhism + Jainism coexisted |
| **Caste** | Did NOT develop as rigidly as North India; Varna system came later |
| **Dress** | Rich: muslin, silk; Common: cotton; Women adorned hair with flowers |
| **Arts** | Musical instruments: drum, flute, yazh; Koothu (folk drama); Muthamizh = Iyal+Isai+Naatakam |
| **Festivals** | Pongal (harvest), Karthigai (spring), Indira vizha (capital) |
| **Games** | Bull fights, cock fights, wrestling, dice, hunting |
| **Minor chieftains** | Ay (Ayar = shepherd origin), Velir/Vellalars (land-owning), Kizhar (village chief) |
| **Seven Patrons** | Kadaiyezhu Vallalgal: Pari, Kari, Ori, Pegan, Ay, Adiyaman, Nalli |

### Sangam Economy & Trade

```
Trade Levels:
    ├── LOCAL — barter in villages
    ├── OVERLAND — bullock carts; caravans
    └── OVERSEAS — sea-going vessels (Kalam, Pahri, Odam, Toni, Teppam, Navai)

Markets (Madurai):
    ├── Nalangadi = morning bazaar
    └── Allangadi = evening bazaar

Major Ports: Musiri (Muziris) · Tondi · Korkai · Arikamedu · Puhar

Exports: Salt, Pepper, Pearls, Ivory, Silk, Diamonds, Muslin, Sandalwood
Imports: Topaz, Tin, Wine, Glass, Horses

Trade Partners: Greece, Rome, Egypt, China, South East Asia, Sri Lanka
```

> **Muziris** — called "First Emporium of India" by Roman writer Pliny the Elder

### Kallanai Dam

| | Detail |
|---|---|
| Builder | Karikalan (Chola) |
| River | Kaveri |
| Type | Stone dyke (meaning: "stone dam") |
| Purpose | Divert water for irrigation |
| Area irrigated | ~69,000 acres |

    │   │   1. Nattrinai       2. Kurunthogai
    │   │   3. Paripaadal      4. Pathittrupathu
    │   │   5. Aingurunuru     6. Kalithogai
    │   │   7. Akanaanuru      8. Puranaanuru
    │   │

    │       1. Thirumurugatrupadai    2. Porunaratrupadai
    │       3. Perumpanatruppadai    4. Sirupanatruppadai
    │       5. Mullaipaattu          6. Nedunalvaadai
    │       7. Maduraikanchi         8. Kurinjipaattu
    │       9. Pattinappaalai       10. Malaipadukadam
    │

### Key Literary Facts

| Work | Author | Key Fact |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Tholkappiyam** | Tholkappiyar | Oldest Tamil grammar |
| **Thirukkural** | Thiruvalluvar | 1330 couplets; "No literature equal to this in the world" — Albert Schweitzer |
| **Silappathikaram** | Ilango Adigal (Cheran Senguttuvan's brother) | Epic of Kovalan & Kannagi; mentions Jallikattu (Mullai Kali) |

| **Pathittrupathu** | — | About Chera kings |

> Ilango Adigal = younger brother of Cheran Senguttuvan

## SECTION 7 — Medieval Tamil Society [TNPSC] [UPSC-M]

### Kalabhras — Dark Interlude

```
Kalabhras (3rd–6th CE):
→ Occupied Tamil country for ~250 years after Sangam decline
→ Very little information — no artefacts or monuments
→ Literary sources: Tamil NavalarCharithai, Periapuranam
→ Works written: Seevaka Chinthamani, Kundalakesi
→ Jainism and Buddhism became prominent
→ Script: Vattezhuththu (Sanskrit + Prakrit influence)
→ Trade & commerce continued → NOT a total "dark age"
```

### Medieval Kingdoms — Key Facts

| Dynasty | Period | Capital | Key Contribution |
|---------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Pallavas** | 6th–9th CE | Kanchipuram | Cave sculptures; Mahabalipuram; patronised Dravidian architecture |
| **Medieval Pandyas** | 6th–14th CE | Madurai | Kazhugumalai monolithic rock-cut cave temple |
| **Imperial Cholas** | 9th–13th CE | Thanjavur, Gangaikondacholapuram | Brihadeeswara Temple; Tanjore paintings; naval power |
| **Vijayanagara** | 14th–16th CE | Vijayanagara (Hampi) | Temple architecture; gopurams; Tamil temple development |

### Pallava Contribution [TNPSC — Frequently Asked]

```
Pallavas → Cave sculptures (patronised):
→ Shore Temple, Mahabalipuram
→ Pancha Rathas (monolithic)
→ Varaha Cave Temple
→ Rock-cut mandapas

Script: Grantha script developed under Pallavas
Art school: Pallava school → influenced Southeast Asian art
```

### Chola Period Arts [TNPSC]

```
Tanjore Paintings → flourished under CHOLAS
Reasons for Tanjore paintings' growth:
→ Chola royal patronage
→ Proximity to Brihadeeswara temple (Peruvudayar)
→ Rich temple culture and devotional art tradition
→ Gold leaf, precious stones, vibrant colours = distinctive style
```

## SECTION 8 — Arts, Literature & Culture [TNPSC] [UPSC-M]

### Dravidian Languages — Identity [TNPSC MAINS]

```
Dravidian Languages Set Apart from Other Indian Languages:
→ Distinguished from Indo-Aryan languages
→ Rev. Robert Caldwell (1856): "A Comparative Grammar of the Dravidian 
  or South Indian Family of Languages" → gave birth to Dravidian concept
→ Tamil is the OLDEST surviving classical language
→ Agglutinative structure (words built by adding suffixes)
→ No Sanskrit-origin base (unlike Hindi/Bengali)
→ Features: Retroflex sounds, dravidian morphology, unique script
→ Tamil (classical) → 2004: Declared Classical Language by Govt of India
```

## SECTION 9 — Dravidian Movement [TNPSC] [UPSC-M]

### Timeline — Dravidian Movement

```
1856 → Rev. Robert Caldwell publishes Comparative Grammar of Dravidian Languages
         → Gives birth to Dravidian concept
              ↓
1891 → Dravida Mahajana Sabha formed (first conference: Salem)
              ↓
1912 → Madras United League → renamed Madras Dravidian Association
         (Dr. C. Natesa Mudaliar's role)
              ↓
1916 → JUSTICE PARTY formed (South Indian Liberal Federation)
         Founders: T.M. Nair + Thiyagaraya Chetty
         Newspaper: 'Justice' (English) + 'Dravidian' (Tamil) + 'Andhra Prakasika' (Telugu)
              ↓
1920 → Justice Party wins 63/98 seats (Montague-Chelmsford Reforms elections)
         First CM: A. Subbarayalu Reddiar
              ↓
1925 → PERIYAR starts Self-Respect Movement (Erode)
         Left Congress in 1925 over caste discrimination
              ↓
1944 → Periyar transforms Justice Party → DRAVIDAR KAZHAGAM (DK)
         (Salem conference) → withdrew from electoral politics
              ↓
1949 → C.N. Annadurai splits from DK → forms DRAVIDA MUNNETRA KAZHAGAM (DMK)
```

### Justice Party — Key Facts

| | Detail |
|---|---|
| Official name | South Indian Liberal Federation |
| Founded | 1916 |
| Founders | T.M. Nair + Thiyagaraya Chetty |
| Purpose | Non-Brahmin movement; representation in public services |
| First ministry | A. Subbarayalu Reddiar (1920) |
| In power | ~13 years in Madras Presidency |
| Newspapers | Justice (English), Dravidian (Tamil) |
| Achievement | Communal GO (1921) — reservation for non-Brahmins |
| Decline | Congress opposition + caste/wealth divides + economic depression |

### Periyar E.V. Ramasamy [TNPSC — Frequently Asked]

```
Periyar:
→ Full name: Erode Venkatappa Ramasamy
→ Left Congress: 1925 (over caste discrimination at Vaikom)
→ Started Self-Respect Movement: 1925, ERODE
→ Journals: Kudiarasu, Viduthalai, Revolt (English)
→ Transformed Justice Party into Dravidar Kazhagam: 1944 (Salem)
→ Dravidar Kazhagam = social organization (not political party)
```

### C.N. Annadurai (Anna)

```
→ Attracted to Self-Respect Movement → joined Dravidar Kazhagam
→ Broke from Periyar → formed DMK (1949)
→ Used cinema to spread ideals (1940s – cinema became popular)
→ Works: Velaikkari (novel)
→ Elected to Rajya Sabha: 1968
→ Became first CM of Tamil Nadu under DMK: 1967
→ Renamed Madras State to TAMIL NADU: 1969
```
