## SECTION 5 — TNPSC Notes [TNPSC]

### A. Thirukkural as Political Philosophy ★★★

```
THIRUKKURAL — PORUTPAAL (Book 2: Wealth / Governance)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Porutpaal = Chapters 39 to 95 (out of 133 total)
Key sections relevant to political thought:

ARASIYAL (Governance):
  → King must protect subjects like a shepherd
  → Three qualities of a king: Kottu (valor), Kodumai (justice),
    Kaval (protection)
  → Bad king = drought, disease, poverty for people
  → "The king IS the rain, the king IS the land" — people depend on king

AMAICHAR (Ministers / Council):
  → Good ministers = king's greatest strength
  → Must be learned, loyal, wise, and incorruptible
  → Seek counsel before acting; avoid sycophants

THOODHU (Diplomacy / Ambassadors):
  → Importance of skilled envoys
  → Diplomat = backbone of foreign policy
  → Non-violence preferred; war is the last resort

PADAIMAADUMAI (Military Strength):
  → Standing army needed; soldiers must be well-fed and motivated

NEETHI (Justice):
  → Justice = foundation of the state
  → A king who is unjust loses legitimacy
  → Justice must be equal for poor and rich — no discrimination

ARASI (Economics / Wealth):
  → State must collect taxes fairly — "as bee takes honey without hurting the flower"
  → Excessive taxation destroys wealth; minimal taxation builds prosperity

UNIVERSAL ASPECT OF THIRUKKURAL:
  → No mention of God, caste, or religion in governance sections
  → Universal humanist framework: "Yaadum Oore Yaavarum Kelir"
    (Every place is my home; every person is my kin)
  → Translated into 80+ languages worldwide
```

### B. Justice Party (1916) — Deep Notes ★★★

```
JUSTICE PARTY — SOUTH INDIAN LIBERAL FEDERATION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

BACKGROUND:
  → Early 20th century: Brahmins held disproportionate share of
    government jobs + education in Madras Presidency
  → Non-Brahmin elite (wealthy landowners, traders, professionals)
    demanded political representation

FOUNDING:
  → Date: November 20, 1916 (in Madras)
  → Key founders:
    ★★★ Dr. T.M. Nair (physician + politician)
    ★★★ P. Theagaroya Chetty (lawyer; first president)
    Alamelu Mangai Thayarammaal (women's representation)
  → Name "Justice" from their English newspaper

NON-BRAHMIN MANIFESTO (1916):
  → Demanded representation for non-Brahmin communities
  → Challenged Brahmin monopoly on ICS, judiciary, education
  → Supported Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919)

KEY ACHIEVEMENTS:
  1. Communal G.O. (1921) — FIRST RESERVATION IN INDIA ★★★
     → Madras Presidency government jobs reserved for non-Brahmins
     → Percentages fixed for different communities
     → Basis for Tamil Nadu's reservation system today
  2. Elected to Madras Legislative Council (under Montagu-Chelmsford)
  3. Governed Madras Presidency 1920–1926, 1930–1937 (with British support)

IDEOLOGY:
  → Anti-Brahminism (social, not personal)
  → Non-Brahmin solidarity across castes
  → Moderate nationalism (cooperated with British unlike INC)
  → Social justice + representation

DECLINE:
  → Could not mobilise masses; elite-dominated party
  → Congress under Gandhi had mass appeal
  → Periyar took over → renamed DK in 1944

LEGACY:
  → Foundation of Dravidian movement
  → Reservation system in TN originates here
  → Non-Brahmin assertion became basis of Dravidian politics
```

### C. Self-Respect Movement (1925) — Periyar ★★★

```
SELF-RESPECT MOVEMENT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

FOUNDER: E.V. Ramasamy Naicker (PERIYAR)
YEAR: 1925
HEADQUARTERS: Erode, later Madras (Chennai)

BACKGROUND:
  → Periyar joined INC (1919); active in Vaikom Satyagraha (Kerala, 1924-25)
  → At Congress session (1925) in Kanchipuram: Brahmins served food,
    non-Brahmins not allowed at same line → Periyar walked out
  → Founded Self-Respect Movement to end such social discrimination

CORE PRINCIPLES:
  1. RATIONALISM (Periythuvam):
     → Reject religious superstition; accept only reason and science
     → Challenge all religious texts that justify inequality
     → "Think before you believe; believe only what makes rational sense"

  2. ATHEISM:
     → Gods and religious concepts used to subjugate lower castes
     → Periyar burned images of Ganesha (Kancheepuram, 1971)
     → "There is no God; the inventor of God is a fool;
        the propagator of God is a scoundrel;
        the worshipper of God is a barbarian."

  3. ANTI-BRAHMINISM:
     → Brahminism = ideology that justifies hierarchy; not all Brahmins
     → Challenged Manusmriti (burned publicly)
     → Opposed Sanskrit-based rituals that excluded Dalits

  4. WOMEN'S LIBERATION (Pen Viduthalai):
     → Supported widow remarriage
     → Opposed devadasi system
     → Advocated for birth control (ahead of his time — 1930s!)
     → Self-Respect marriages: no priest, voluntary union
     → Equal inheritance rights for women
     → Women can be rational agents — need not accept religious subjugation

  5. CASTE ABOLITION:
     → Varna + Jati = mechanisms of exploitation
     → Opposed untouchability in all forms
     → "The untouchables will be free only when they have political power"

  6. INTER-CASTE MARRIAGES:
     → Personally conducted hundreds of self-respect marriages
     → Married Maniammai in 1948 (she was 40 years younger) — set example

ORGANISATIONS:
  → Self-Respect Movement (1925)
  → Kudi Arasu (magazine, 1925) — Tamil
  → Viduthalai (newspaper, 1935) — voice of liberation

DRAVIDAR KAZHAGAM (DK) — 1944:
  → Periyar renamed Justice Party as DK
  → More radical; electoral abstention initially
  → "Vote for none — all parties are the same"
  → Black flag protests; anti-Hindu processions
  → DK remains active today (non-electoral)

PERIYAR'S LEGACY:
  → "Thanthai Periyar" = Father of Dravidian Movement
  → UNESCO: "Prophet of New Age, Socrates of South East Asia,
    Father of Social Reform Movement and Eradicator of Ignorance" (1970) ★★★
  → Statue in all TN government offices
  → September 17 = Periyar Jayanti (government holiday in TN)
  → Reservation system in TN = direct legacy
  → Self-Respect Marriage Law: Tamil Nadu Marriages (Registration) Act (now
    legally valid without priest — Self-Respect Marriages)
```

### D. Anti-Hindi Agitation ★★★

```
ANTI-HINDI AGITATIONS — TWO PHASES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

FIRST ANTI-HINDI AGITATION (1937–1940)

CAUSE:
  → Rajagopalachari (Rajaji) — Congress CM of Madras Presidency
  → Introduced compulsory Hindi in government schools (1937)
  → Non-Tamil people can't be forced to learn Hindi — language imposition
  → Periyar and DK leaders opposed vehemently

LEADERS:
  → Periyar (E.V. Ramasamy Naicker)
  → C.N. Annadurai (future DMK founder — rose to prominence here)
  → Arinjar Anna rose as orator through this agitation

EVENTS:
  → Mass hartals, bandhs, street protests
  → Women participated in large numbers
  → Two deaths: Natarajan and Aasaan (in police firing) ★★
  → Rajaji called protestors "anti-national"

RESULT:
  → British government withdrew the Hindi compulsory order (1940)
  → Victory for Tamil identity
  → Periyar and Anna became heroes

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECOND ANTI-HINDI AGITATION (1965)

CAUSE:
  → Constitution (Art. 343): Hindi to be official language after 15 years
  → Deadline: January 26, 1965
  → Union government (Lal Bahadur Shastri) intended to make Hindi sole
    official language; English to be removed

LEADERS:
  → C.N. Annadurai (DMK)
  → M. Karunanidhi (DMK)
  → P. Nedunchezhiyan, E.V.K. Sampath (DMK leaders)

EVENTS:
  → Widespread protests across TN
  → Self-immolations: first by Chinnasami (Tirupur student)
  → Deaths: 70+ people died (police firing + self-immolations) ★★
  → Two DMK MPs Muthu (Kanchipuram) and Nanjil Manoharan immolated
  → Central government: imposed President's Rule in TN (briefly)

RESULT:
  → Lal Bahadur Shastri gave assurance — English will continue
  → Official Languages Amendment Act (1967) — "as long as non-Hindi
    states want, English will remain associate official language"
  → Hindi never imposed on South Indian states ★★★
  → DMK rode this wave to victory in 1967 elections
  → First non-Congress government in any major Indian state

SIGNIFICANCE OF BOTH AGITATIONS:
  → Laid foundation for federal linguistic rights
  → Three-Language Formula emerged (regional language + Hindi + English)
  → Defined TN's political identity as protector of Tamil language
  → Made language identity central to Dravidian politics
```

### E. C.N. Annadurai (Anna) — DMK ★★★

```
C.N. ANNADURAI — "ANNA" (Elder Brother)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PERSONAL DETAILS:
  Full name: Conjeevaram Natarajan Annadurai
  Born: September 15, 1909, Kancheepuram
  Died: February 3, 1969, Madras (CM in office)
  Title: "Anna" (elder brother) — given by the masses

POLITICAL CAREER:
  → Joined DK under Periyar
  → Rose to prominence in 1937 Anti-Hindi agitation
  → Split from DK over: electoral participation vs Periyar's abstention
  → Founded DMK: September 17, 1949

DMK — DRAVIDA MUNNETRA KAZHAGAM (1949):
  → "Dravida" = Dravidian (South Indian identity)
  → "Munnetra" = Progress
  → "Kazhagam" = Association/Party
  → Entered electoral politics (unlike DK)
  → Initially demanded DRAVIDA NADU (separate South India nation) ★★
  → Later DROPPED Dravida Nadu demand (before 1962 elections —
    Anna declared demand dropped to contest within Indian Union framework)

IDEOLOGY:
  → Dravidian nationalism: Tamil identity + social justice
  → Rationalism (inherited from Periyar but softer)
  → Social justice + anti-Brahminism (without Periyar's atheism)
  → Tamil pride + cultural identity
  → Welfare state for the poor

AS POLITICIAN:
  → Master orator, playwright, and writer
  → Wrote plays with social messages
  → Used cultural programs (drama, music) for political mobilisation

AS CM (1967–1969):
  → First DMK CM; first non-Congress CM in any major Indian state ★★★
  → Reduced rice prices immediately
  → Abolished Hindi requirement in schools
  → Started social welfare schemes
  → Died of oral cancer at 60 (February 3, 1969) — mourned nationally
  → Death: unprecedented mourning; 15 million attended funeral

LEGACY:
  → Transformed South Indian politics
  → Proved regional parties can govern effectively
  → Made Tamil pride and social justice mainstream
  → February 3 = Anna Memorial Day (government holiday in TN)
```

### F. Subramania Bharati — Political Philosophy ★★

```
SUBRAMANIA BHARATI (1882–1921)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Full name: Chinnaswami Subramania Bharati
Born: December 11, 1882, Ettayapuram (Thoothukudi district)
Died: September 11, 1921
Title: "Mahakavi" (Great Poet), "Bharathiyar"

POLITICAL THOUGHT:
  1. NATIONALISM:
     → Fierce advocate of Indian independence
     → Attended Surat Congress (1907) with Bal Gangadhar Tilak
     → Influenced by Tilak's assertive nationalism AND Aurobindo's spiritualism
     → Wrote "Bharathamata" (songs for Mother India)
     → "Vande Mataram" spirit in Tamil

  2. WOMEN'S LIBERATION (decades ahead):
     → Songs about free, educated, independent women
     → "Pudhumai Penn" (The New Woman) — concept of liberated woman
     → Opposed purdah, child marriage, widowhood restrictions
     → Called for women's education, voting rights (before women got vote)
     → "A country where women are enslaved cannot be free"

  3. ANTI-CASTEISM:
     → Openly opposed untouchability
     → Had meals with Dalits (radical act in 1900s)
     → "Jati veri" (casteism) = enemy of Tamil people
     → Wrote songs celebrating the dignity of manual labor

  4. UNIVERSAL BROTHERHOOD:
     → "Yaadum Oore Yaavarum Kelir" philosophy
     → Synthesis of Vedantic universalism + Tamil humanism

  5. ECONOMIC IDEAS:
     → Swadeshi economics — use Indian products
     → Self-reliance as basis of freedom

LIVED IN PONDICHERRY (1908–1918):
  → Escaped British arrest warrants
  → Under French protection; continued political writing
  → Corresponded with Aurobindo Ghosh

WORKS:
  → "Panchali Sabatham" (Draupadi's oath — political allegory)
  → "Kuyil Pattu" (Song of the Koel)
  → "Kannan Pattu" (Songs of Krishna)
  → Nationalist songs, women's liberation poems
```

### G. V.O. Chidambaram Pillai (VOC) ★★

```
V.O. CHIDAMBARAM PILLAI — "KAPPALOTTIYA TAMILAN"
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Title: "Kappalottiya Tamilan" = "The Tamil who steered ships" ★★★
Born: September 5, 1872, Ottapidaram (near Thoothukudi)
Died: November 18, 1936

SWADESHI STEAM NAVIGATION COMPANY (1906): ★★★
  → Founded to challenge British monopoly in Tuticorin–Colombo sea route
  → British India Steam Navigation Company (BISN) had monopoly
  → VOC collected funds from Tamil merchants + laborers
  → SS Gallia ship launched
  → Impact: freight charges reduced; Tamils had economic agency
  → British responded: VOC and colleagues arrested (1908)

TIRUNELVELI RIOTS (1908):
  → Workers at Coral Mills went on strike (led by VOC)
  → British opened fire: 2 killed (Subramania Siva + others protested)
  → VOC arrested for sedition

IMPRISONMENT:
  → Convicted of sedition; sentenced to hard labor (rigorous imprisonment)
  → At Coimbatore jail: forced to work grinding oil mill
  → Symbol of resistance through sacrifice

SIGNIFICANCE:
  → Economic nationalism: Indian-owned enterprise challenging British monopoly
  → Labor rights: organized Tinnevelly workers (1908 — among first labor unions in India)
  → Self-reliance before Gandhi's Swadeshi movement went national
  → Tuticorin port renamed "V.O. Chidambaranar Port" in his honor ★★★
```

### I. Maraimalai Adigal — Tamil Purist Movement ★

```
MARAIMALAI ADIGAL (1876–1950)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

"Shuddha Tamil" (Pure Tamil) Movement
  → Remove all Sanskrit-derived loanwords from Tamil
  → Create a pure classical Tamil vocabulary
  → Tamil identity through linguistic purity (not just political resistance)

THOUGHT:
  → Tamil has no need for Sanskrit; Tamil is self-sufficient
  → "Tamil literature predates Vedic literature"
  → Tamil = language of Dravidians; Sanskrit = language of Aryan invaders
  → Dravidian identity rooted in Tamil language pride

SIGNIFICANCE:
  → Started the Tamil Purism (Sentamizh) movement
  → Connected linguistic purity with Dravidian identity
  → His ideas became foundation of Tamil cultural nationalism
  → Influenced TN textbooks and literature policy

### J. Dravidian Movement — Political Philosophy ★★★

```
DRAVIDIAN MOVEMENT — CORE IDEOLOGY
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

THREE PILLARS:
  1. SOCIAL JUSTICE (Samadharma)
     → Representation for all communities in jobs, education, governance
     → Reservation as instrument of social justice (not charity)
     → "Social democracy is more important than political democracy"

  2. TAMIL IDENTITY (Tamilar Urimai)
     → Tamil language = identity + culture + pride
     → Protection of Tamil language against Hindi imposition
     → Tamil as classical language (now officially recognised — 2004) ★★
     → Opposition to linguistic discrimination

  3. RATIONALISM (Periythuvam / Arivu)
     → Reason over religion
     → Scientific temper against superstition
     → Challenge to religious authority that justifies hierarchy
     → Secularism in governance

DRAVIDA NADU DEMAND (1940s–1950s):
  → DMK initially demanded independent Dravida Nadu
  → States Reorganisation Act (1956): linguistic states accepted
  → Anna DROPPED Dravida Nadu demand before 1962 elections
  → "We accept India as our motherland; Tamil Nadu is our home"
  → Remained within Indian Union but protected Tamil identity

DRAVIDIAN POLITICS VS BRAHMINICAL POLITICS:
  Dravidian parties: OBC + Dalit mobilisation; social welfare; secular
  Congress in TN: upper-caste dominated; religious symbolism
  Dravidian win (1967): ushered in welfare state model

SUCCESSIVE LEADERS:
  Periyar (ideological father) → Anna (mass mobiliser + CM) →
  Karunanidhi (statesman, literary figure, long-serving CM) →
  MGR (welfare populism, cinema politics) → Jayalalithaa →
  M.K. Stalin (current CM, DMK)
```

## SECTION 6 — UPSC Mains Notes [UPSC-M]

### Introduction for UPSC Essay

> Tamil Nadu's political thought is a unique synthesis of ancient humanist ethics (Thirukkural), colonial-era rationalism (Periyar), democratic socialism (Anna), and Dalit assertion (Ayothidass). It challenged the caste order long before Ambedkar's constitutional framework arrived, creating a distinctive "Dravidian model" of governance rooted in social justice.

### UPSC GS I Framework — Social Reform Movements

```
CONTRIBUTION OF TN THINKERS TO INDIA'S REFORM MOVEMENT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. CHALLENGING CASTE HIERARCHY (before Independence):
   → Ayothidass Pandithar (1898): Buddhism as Dalit liberation tool
   → Periyar (1925): rationalism as weapon against caste
   → TN's non-Brahmin movement predates many all-India reforms
   → Communal G.O. 1921: first affirmative action in India

B. WOMEN'S LIBERATION:
   → Bharati (1882-1921): "Pudhumai Penn" concept
   → Periyar: birth control, widow remarriage, self-respect marriages
   → Justice Party: included women (Alamelu Mangai Thayarammaal)
   → TN's women's movement = vanguard for all-India women's rights

C. LINGUISTIC IDENTITY AND FEDERALISM:
   → Anti-Hindi agitations shaped India's language policy
   → 1965 agitation: Made English continue as official language
   → Three-Language Formula: indirect outcome of TN's resistance
   → Constitutional safeguard: Art. 347 + Art. 351 — balance of Hindi promotion and state language rights

D. DALIT ASSERTION:
   → TN's Dalit movement has distinct Buddhist + Tamil identity link
   → Pre-dates Ambedkar; connects with him post-1940s
   → Reservation in TN (OBC 50%+, SC 18%, ST 1%) = world's largest reservation system by percentage

E. RATIONALISM AND SECULARISM:
   → Periyar's movement = only major political movement based on atheism
   → Tamil Nadu government = most secular state government in India
   → No state-sponsored religious events; rationalist education
```

### UPSC Mains Model Answer Framework

```
Q: "The Dravidian movement represented a unique challenge to
    social hierarchy in India. Critically assess."

INTRO:
  The Dravidian movement, emerging in early 20th century Tamil Nadu,
  was distinct from other reform movements — it simultaneously
  challenged religious authority, caste hierarchy, linguistic imposition,
  and political monopoly, creating a model that influenced India's
  social democracy.

BODY:
  Positive contributions:
  1. Communal G.O. (1921) — first reservation → template for Constitution
  2. Anti-Hindi agitation → protected linguistic federalism
  3. Women's liberation — ahead of national mainstream
  4. Rational-secular politics — not Hindu majoritarianism
  5. 1967 governance — proved regional parties can deliver welfare

  Limitations:
  1. Dravida Nadu demand — divisive initially
  2. Periyar's atheism alienated religious masses
  3. Caste-based politics: communities still vote on caste lines
  4. "Dravidian parties" eventually became dynastic
  5. Within Dravidian movement: Dalits still marginalised

CONCLUSION:
  TN's Dravidian model established that democracy can be a tool for
  social transformation, not just political change. Its legacy —
  reservation, women's rights, language rights, secular governance —
  shaped modern India's constitutional democracy.
```

### Periyar vs Ambedkar — Comparison [UPSC-M]

PERIYAR vs AMBEDKAR — SIMILARITIES AND DIFFERENCES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SIMILARITIES:
  Both: Challenged caste system; fought for Dalit/OBC rights
  Both: Rejected Hinduism as oppressive system
  Both: Advocated for women's rights; education for lower castes
  Both: Used political action (not just social reform) to achieve change

  → Pure rationalism/atheism (no religion at all)

  → Constitutional safeguards = primary tool

  Where they differed:
  → On Gandhi: Both opposed (Poona Pact 1932 controversy)
  → On Congress: Both opposed but differently
  → On Islam: Ambedkar wrote critical analysis; Periyar was more pluralist
  → On women: Both progressive but Periyar more radical

COMBINED LEGACY IN TN:
  → Dravidian + Ambedkarite strands both strong in TN

WHO HE WAS:

### Three-Language Formula and TN [TNPSC] [UPSC-M]

```
THREE-LANGUAGE FORMULA — BACKGROUND AND TN RESISTANCE
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ORIGIN:
  → Kothari Commission (1964-66) recommended Three-Language Formula
  → Study: mother tongue + Hindi + English
  → OR: In Hindi-speaking states: Hindi + English + a modern Indian language
  → Goal: national integration + regional language preservation

TN's POSITION:
  → TN follows TWO-language formula: Tamil + English (no Hindi)
  → Rejected Hindi as third language since 1965 agitation
  → DMK, AIADMK, all TN parties united on this
  → NEP 2020 controversy: TN felt it reintroduced Hindi through back door
    → CM M.K. Stalin sent letter to PM Modi opposing Hindi imposition
    → Central govt clarified: Three-language formula; no compulsory Hindi
